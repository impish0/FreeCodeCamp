Single Shade Of Grey: 5 Greate Ways To Deal With The Long Big Lake Winters

The Big Lake winter. It’s a beast, isn’t it? A monochrome monolith of grey – snow, ice, and the occasional grumpy resident. But fear not, weary traveler! Don’t let the darkness steal your joy. Assemble a “Cozy Kit” – think flannel pajamas, a ridiculously oversized mug, a box of gourmet dark chocolate, and a good book. Dust off those knitting needles (a scarf? A gnome?) or try pottery. Gather friends for games or cozy up with “hygge” – candles, warm drinks, and loved ones. Bundle up and brave the snow, or just enjoy the warmth and company within. Don’t let the grey win!

The Big Lake Biscuit Bonanza!

Don’t let the winter blues ruin your appetite! This year’s Big Lake Biscuit Bonanza is serving up mountains of fluffy, buttery biscuits alongside a dazzling array of toppings – honey, maple syrup, chocolate, fruit preserves, even a decadent chocolate cream! It’s the perfect way to warm up, gather with friends and family, and celebrate the simple pleasures of a perfectly good biscuit. Proceeds benefit the Big Lake Community Kitchen, so enjoy a treat and support a great cause!

Last Week In Review

Big-Time Falling Into Place Around The Lake

The annual Biglake Fall Festival is shaping up to be the biggest yet! Volunteers are diligently constructing the new lakeside trails, and local businesses are scrambling to prepare for the anticipated surge of tourists. Organizers are particularly excited about the installation of interactive digital displays designed to educate visitors about the lake's unique ecosystem and, of course, highlight the stunning views. “We’re thrilled to welcome families and outdoor enthusiasts to Big Lake,” says festival director, Mayor Beatrice Plum. “This year, we’re focusing on sustainable tourism and showcasing the natural beauty that makes Big Lake so special.” Don't miss the pumpkin carving contest or the live bluegrass music!

3 Ways To Celebrate Halloween In Big Lake

Get ready to spook and delight! Big Lake is gearing up for a fantastically frightful Halloween. Here are three ways to make the most of the spooky season: 1) Decorate your front yard with creatively terrifying displays. 2) Organize a neighborhood trick-or-treating extravaganza. 3) Join the annual Big Lake Costume Parade – prizes will be awarded for the most original and impressive costumes. Little ghouls and goblins, prepare for a night of sugar, laughter, and unforgettable memories!

Churches? We Got 5...

Big Lake is blessed with a vibrant and welcoming religious community. Here are five of the finest places of worship in town, each offering a unique and comforting atmosphere. St. Jude’s offers traditional services, while the Harmony Chapel focuses on contemporary worship. The Old Stone Church provides a historical setting, and the Lakeside Meditation Center offers a space for quiet reflection. Finally, the Community Fellowship welcomes everyone to their weekly services. “We’re proud to serve the spiritual needs of our community,” says Reverend Silas Blackwood. “ Whether you're looking for solace, connection, or simply a moment of peace, Big Lake has a church for you.”
