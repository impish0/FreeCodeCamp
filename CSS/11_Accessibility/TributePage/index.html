Alan Turing

The father of computer science and artificial intelligence

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England
  • 1926 - Enters Sherborne School, where his passion for mathematics and science becomes evident despite the school's emphasis on classics
  • 1931 - Enrolls at King's College, Cambridge, to study mathematics
  • 1934 - Graduates with first-class honors in mathematics from Cambridge
  • 1935 - Elected a fellow of King's College at age 22 for his work on probability theory
  • 1936 - Publishes "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Turing machine - a theoretical device that became the foundation of computer science
  • 1938 - Receives his Ph.D. from Princeton University, where he studied under Alonzo Church
  • 1939 - Returns to Cambridge and is recruited by the Government Code and Cypher School at the outbreak of World War II
  • 1940 - Arrives at Bletchley Park, the secret British code-breaking center, where he works on breaking the German Enigma code
  • 1941 - Develops the Bombe machine, which significantly speeds up the decryption of Enigma-encoded messages, potentially shortening the war by 2-4 years
  • 1942 - Travels to the United States to share code-breaking techniques with American cryptographers
  • 1945 - Joins the National Physical Laboratory and begins work on the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE), one of the first designs for a stored-program computer
  • 1948 - Moves to the University of Manchester to work on the Manchester Mark 1 computer
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the famous Turing Test to determine machine intelligence
  • 1951 - Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society for his contributions to mathematics and computer science
  • 1952 - Prosecuted for homosexuality, which was illegal in Britain at the time. Accepts chemical castration as an alternative to prison
  • 1954 - Dies at age 41 from cyanide poisoning, ruled suicide by the coroner
  • 2009 - British Prime Minister Gordon Brown issues a public apology for the government's treatment of Turing
  • 2013 - Receives a royal pardon from Queen Elizabeth II
